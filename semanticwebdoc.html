A Little About Me:

My journey into loving to read began quite quickly in my memory. After learning the letters of the alphabet and their phonetic sounds, I started piecing them together to sound out words. I eagerly read any signs I came across and soon picked up my first book. However, what truly got me obsessed was Shel Silverstein's "The Giving Tree." After devouring many more of his works, I graduated to chapter books by 2nd grade, and from there, my passion for reading only grew. I always had a "fun read" in addition to whatever required reading my class had in literature. My voracious reading habits were soon noticed, and I was placed into UC Berkeley's summer program for gifted children for six years, until I was old enough to rebel against elective summer school. Over the years, I have devoured everything from classics like "Wuthering Heights" to cult classics like "Harry Potter" and "Twilight," and more niche books like "Kafka on the Shore." My favorite of all genres are historical books, I don't discriminate from fiction to nonfiction.

Book Reviews and Recommendations

I love sharing my thoughts on the books I read. Here are a few of my recent reads and what I think about them:

  • The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald - A timeless classic that captures the essence of the American Dream.
  • 1984 by George Orwell - A chilling dystopian novel that remains relevant today.
  • To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee - A profound narrative on racial injustice and moral growth.
