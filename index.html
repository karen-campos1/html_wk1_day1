The Mid-Atlantic Accent in Old Hollywood

The Mid-Atlantic accent, also known as the Transatlantic accent, is a consciously learned way of speaking English that was popular in the early 20th century. It was primarily used by the American upper class and the entertainment industry. It blends American and British English and was often used by actors in old Hollywood movies to appeal to both American and British audiences.

Timeline of the Mid-Atlantic Accent

A look back in time:
1920s The Mid-Atlantic accent begins to gain popularity in theatrical productions
1930s Hollywood's Golden Age sees widespread use of the accent in films.
1940s The accent starts to decline in popularity post-World War II.
